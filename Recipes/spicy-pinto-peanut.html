Spicy Pinto Peanut Skillet

Description

This is a favorite on the boat. It's a rich combination of flavors with a lot of depth afforded by the addition of peanut butter.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 can pinto beans, drained
  • 1/2 can diced tomatoes with green chilies
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 onion, diced (or 1/2 tsp onion powder)
  • 1–2 tsp peanut butter (smooth or chunky)
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/4 tsp chili flakes (or to taste)
  • Dash soy sauce or salt to taste
  • Small drizzle of honey or brown sugar (balances heat)
  • Cheese (handful, shredded)
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced (for topping)
  • 2 flour tortillas
  • Oil for cooking (olive or vegetable)
  • Lime juice (to finish)
  • Optional: green onion or cilantro

Steps

  1. Sauté the veggies: Heat a bit of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the diced red bell pepper and onion (or onion powder). Sauté for 3–4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add big flavors: Stir in garlic, cumin, and chili powder for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the pinto beans and the tomatoes with green chilies. Stir and simmer for 3–5 minutes.
  4. Add the peanut butter and stir until it melts into the sauce — it’ll get slightly creamy and rich. Adjust a splash of soy sauce (or salt) & drizzle of honey
  5. Add cheese: Sprinkle cheese over the top, cover, and let it melt for a minute.
  6. Fry tortilla: in another pan, heat oil until hot; then add tortilla, move around in oil, and fry to liking (30-60 seconds on each side)
  7. Finish: Move filling to tortilla, top with avocado slices, a squeeze of lime, and optional green onion or cilantro.
